1. Logo

ReformLab

Concept

A bimodal dot histogram — the signature chart of distributional analysis, simplified into a brand mark. Dots represent individuals in a population. The bimodal curve shows two groups affected differently by a reform. Mixed slate and emerald colors represent two overlapping policy outcomes.

Brand mark colors — fixed: Slate 700 (#334155) and Emerald 500 (#10B981).

Wordmark: Inter Semibold (600), Slate 700.

8. Visual Style Rules

Do

  • Flat vector graphics, clean illustrations
  • White or Slate 50 backgrounds
  • Functional 1px borders for structure
  • Line-style icons (Lucide)
  • Monospace for all numbers
  • Slight rounding on interactive components
  • Every visual communicates information

Don't

  • Photography — this is a data product
  • Gradients, textures, or patterns
  • Decorative swooshes or shapes
  • Shadows on static layout elements
  • Filled/solid icons
  • 3D effects on charts
  • Custom animations beyond transitions

9. Brand Voice

Tone: Pragmatic Optimism

Don't oversell. Don't claim to save the world. Claim to make one specific thing work better. "This helps" — not "this changes everything."

Expert

Peer-to-peer, credible
You know this problem. Here's what's finally possible.

Public

Personal, warm, concrete
Let me show you what I'm building and why it matters.

Investor

Confident, evidence-backed
Here's a gap no one has filled in Europe. This is happening.

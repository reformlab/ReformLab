### 2. Cloud & Compute Credit Programs

#### 2.1 AWS Activate

| Tier | Credits | Eligibility | Speed |
|------|---------|-------------|-------|
| **Founders** | $1,000 | Self-funded startups < 10 years, with website | 7-10 business days |
| **Portfolio** | Up to $100,000 | Startups with approved Activate Provider (VC/accelerator) | 7-10 business days |

_Requirements:_ Must have a company (micro-enterprise would work). Company website required. Use corporate email, not Gmail.
_Recommendation:_ **Founders tier ($1,000) is very accessible** if you register a micro-enterprise. Enough for initial backend deployment.
_Source: [AWS Activate](https://aws.amazon.com/activate/activate-landing/), [How to Apply](https://aws.amazon.com/startups/learn/applying-for-aws-activate-credits-a-step-by-step-guide)_

### 3. Grants & Public Funding

#### 3.1 NGI Zero Commons Fund (EU) — HIGHEST PRIORITY

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Amount** | €5,000 – €50,000 (up to €150,000 for exceptional projects) |
| **Total fund** | €21.6 million |
| **Eligibility** | Individuals, SMEs, academics, nonprofits — **no company required** |
| **Current deadline** | **April 1, 2026** (noon CEST) — apply NOW |
| **Geographic** | EU/Horizon Europe countries prioritized (France qualifies) |
| **Requirement** | Must publish as open source |

_Why this is your best fit:_ ReformLab is an open-source tool for public policy — this is exactly what NGI funds. You can apply as an **individual** (no micro-enterprise needed). The deadline is 5 weeks away. Grants of €5k-€50k are realistic for a solo developer.
_Source: [NGI Zero Commons Fund](https://nlnet.nl/commonsfund/), [Open Call Details](https://ngi.eu/opencalls/)_

#### 3.2 BPI France

| Program | Amount | Eligibility | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------------|-------|
| **Bourse French Tech** | Up to €30,000 (subsidy, 50-70% of expenses) | Innovative project, early stage | Requires company structure (micro-enterprise OK) |
| **Bourse French Tech Emergence** | Similar range | DeepTech projects | Requires strong technological content |
| **Aide pour la Faisabilité de l'Innovation** | Varies | Innovation feasibility studies | Requires company |

_Recommendation:_ **Bourse French Tech** is the most accessible — €30k subsidy for a first innovative project. Requires forming a micro-enterprise but application is straightforward.
_Source: [Bourse French Tech](https://www.bpifrance.fr/catalogue-offres/bourse-french-tech), [Innovation Aid](https://les-aides.fr/aide/IClv3w/bpifrance/aide-pour-la-faisabilite-de-l-innovation.html)_

#### 3.3 DINUM — Communs Numériques

The DINUM (Direction interministérielle du numérique) actively supports open-source software ("logiciels libres") and digital commons. They have launched calls for digital commons contributions, and France co-launched the **EDIC Digital Commons** (European Digital Infrastructure Consortium) with Germany, Italy, and the Netherlands in late 2025.

_Recommendation:_ Worth monitoring. ReformLab building on OpenFisca (itself a DINUM-linked project) is a strong narrative fit. Contact code.gouv.fr for current opportunities.
_Source: [DINUM Open Source](https://communs.numerique.gouv.fr/a-propos/), [code.gouv.fr](https://code.gouv.fr/fr/mission/)_

#### 3.4 ADEME

ADEME funds environmental transition projects with a €1+ billion annual budget. They support from research through commercialization via France 2030. Their open data portal (data.ademe.fr) and Transition(s) 2050 scenarios are directly relevant to ReformLab's domain.

_Recommendation:_ Worth exploring for later-stage funding when ReformLab can demonstrate environmental policy impact. Check their [calls for projects](https://www.appelsprojetsrecherche.fr/partenaire/ademe).
_Source: [ADEME Funding](https://www.ademe.fr/en/our-missions/funding/), [Calls for Projects](https://agirpourlatransition.ademe.fr/entreprises/aides-financieres)_

### 4. Ecosystem Sponsorships & Open-Source Foundations

#### 4.1 OpenFisca Community (Open Collective)

OpenFisca itself is funded via Open Collective Europe Foundation. As a project building on OpenFisca, there's potential for collaboration or shared funding.
_Note:_ DINUM stopped directly funding OpenFisca in 2020, pushing toward collective funding by reusers. ReformLab contributing back to OpenFisca could open doors.
_Source: [OpenFisca Open Collective](https://opencollective.com/openfisca), [OpenFisca About](https://openfisca.org/en/about/)_

#### 4.2 GitHub Secure Open Source Fund

$1.25M distributed across 125 projects for security and sustainability of open source. Rolling applications.
_Source: [GitHub Secure Open Source Fund](https://github.com/open-source/github-secure-open-source-fund)_

#### 4.3 NumFOCUS

Fiscal sponsorship for open-source scientific computing projects. Small Development Grants available for community growth, usability improvements, and releases.
_Recommendation:_ ReformLab could apply for NumFOCUS affiliation given its scientific computing nature. This opens access to small grants and community credibility.
_Source: [NumFOCUS](https://numfocus.org/), [Fiscal Sponsorship](https://github.com/numfocus/fiscal-sponsorship)_

#### 4.4 GitHub Sponsors

Individual developers can receive direct funding from supporters. No application — just set up your profile.
_Recommendation:_ Set up GitHub Sponsors on the ReformLab repo. Low effort, long-term passive funding potential.
_Source: [GitHub Sponsors](https://github.com/sponsors)_

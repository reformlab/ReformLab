## General

### What is ReformLab?

An open-source platform for policy impact analysis. It connects European open data to policy simulation models and produces distributional results — who benefits, who loses, how the targeting works — without requiring users to code or build data pipelines from scratch.

### Who is it for?

Three primary audiences:

- **Policy analysts in government** who need to assess the impact of environmental and social policies but don't have time or technical skills to build custom analysis pipelines
- **Researchers and economists** who need reproducible microsimulation results for publications and want a clean Python API instead of ad-hoc scripts
- **Evaluation teams** who want to iterate on policy scenarios without outsourcing every assessment to external researchers

### What kinds of policies can it model?

MVP focuses on environmental and distributional policy for French households:

- Carbon taxes with various redistribution mechanisms
- Energy renovation subsidies
- Vehicle and appliance feebates
- Targeted social transfers
- Any policy expressible as a parameter overlay on OpenFisca tax-benefit calculations

The template system is extensible — you can define custom policy models beyond the built-in templates.

### Is it free?

Yes. ReformLab is open-source. Free to use, free to modify, free to contribute to.

## Data and Privacy

### What data does it use?

By default, ReformLab works entirely with publicly available data: INSEE published tables, Eurostat aggregates, EU-SILC public-use files, emission factor databases. No restricted microdata is required for any core workflow.

### Can I use my own (restricted) data?

Yes. The platform can ingest custom datasets. When you supply restricted microdata, it stays on your machine — the platform never copies, transmits, or persists your data beyond your local environment. Input data paths are referenced, not embedded.

### How are synthetic populations generated?

From published marginal distributions using established statistical methods (iterative proportional fitting, combinatorial optimization). The generation process is fully documented: which source tables, which demographic targets, what tolerances were applied. No hidden defaults.

## Technical

### What's the relationship with OpenFisca?

OpenFisca is the tax-benefit computation engine that ReformLab builds on. OpenFisca encodes legislation and computes individual/household-level effects. ReformLab provides everything above that: data preparation, scenario management, multi-year orchestration, vintage tracking, indicators, and user interfaces.

Think of it this way: OpenFisca is the engine. ReformLab is the car — dashboard, navigation, and all.

### Do I need to know Python?

No. The no-code GUI lets you select data, configure scenarios, run simulations, and view results entirely in the browser. Python is available for users who want full programmability, but it's not required.

### Does it work offline?

Yes. All computation runs locally. No network calls, no cloud dependencies. The platform runs on a standard laptop with 16GB RAM.

### How large a population can it handle?

MVP targets up to 500,000 households on a 16GB laptop. Core computations are vectorized (NumPy/Arrow), so 100k+ households complete in seconds.

### What output formats are supported?

- Visual charts in the GUI and Jupyter notebooks
- CSV and Parquet for data export
- JSON for run manifests
- YAML for scenario configurations

## Reproducibility

### What is a run manifest?

An automatically generated record of everything about a simulation run: engine version, dependency versions, input data hashes, all policy parameters, all synthetic population assumptions, timestamp. It's created as a side effect of running any simulation — no manual documentation needed.

### Can I reproduce someone else's results?

Yes, if they share their run manifest and scenario configuration. Install ReformLab, load their manifest, run it — you get identical results. This is by design: deterministic execution means same inputs always produce same outputs.

### Why does reproducibility matter?

Policy analysis is only credible if others can verify it. When a director questions an assumption, you should be able to show exactly what was used and re-run with an alternative in minutes. When a co-author needs to replicate your results, they shouldn't need to debug your scripts.

## Comparison

### How is this different from PolicyEngine?

PolicyEngine is a web-native policy calculator focused on the US and UK. ReformLab focuses on continental Europe (starting with France), supports environmental policy templates beyond tax-benefit, includes multi-year dynamic projections with vintage tracking, and provides both a no-code GUI and a full Python API. PolicyEngine proved the model — we're bringing it to a market they don't serve.

### How is this different from EUROMOD?

EUROMOD is the EU institutional standard for tax-benefit microsimulation — powerful, well-established, and widely used in research. It's desktop-based, tightly coupled to EU-SILC restricted data, and requires significant training. ReformLab is web-based, works with open data by default, includes environmental policy beyond tax-benefit, and is designed for self-service use by analysts who aren't microsimulation specialists.

### Why not just use OpenFisca directly?

You can. OpenFisca is an excellent computation engine. But using it for a complete policy assessment means building your own data pipeline, writing your own scenario management, implementing your own multi-year orchestration, creating your own indicators, and documenting your own methodology. ReformLab is the product layer that does all of this for you.

### Why not use R / Stata / Excel?

These are general-purpose tools. They work, but you rebuild everything from scratch for every analysis: data loading, cleaning, joining, simulation logic, indicator computation, chart generation, documentation. ReformLab provides a purpose-built pipeline that handles the repetitive infrastructure so you can focus on the policy questions.

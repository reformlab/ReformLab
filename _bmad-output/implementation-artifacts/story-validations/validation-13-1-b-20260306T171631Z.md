## 🚨 Critical Issues (Must Fix)

### 1. Story is too large and complex for a single unit of work.

**Impact:** Increased risk of incomplete delivery within a sprint, higher bug count due to rushed integration, reduced code quality, and potential for delayed subsequent stories (13.2, 13.3, 13.4). The complexity of integrating runtime extensibility across multiple core modules is often underestimated.
**Source:** INVEST - Small, Estimation Reality-Check

**Problem:**
The story encompasses significant architectural changes to fundamental components (`PolicyType` extensibility, global registration, YAML parsing, portfolio integration, orchestrator verification). This breadth and depth of change, especially involving a shift from an enum to a dynamic system, is likely to exceed the capacity of a single sprint, leading to potential delivery failures and quality issues.

**Recommended Fix:**
Break this story into at least two smaller, more manageable stories.
- **Story A: `PolicyType` Extensibility & Registration:** Focus solely on refactoring `PolicyType` to support runtime registration and implementing the `register_policy_type()` and `register_custom_template()` APIs in `schema.py`, including internal validation and test cleanup mechanisms.
- **Story B: Custom Template Integration:** Focus on integrating the newly extensible `PolicyType` and registered custom templates into YAML loading/serialization, portfolio composition, and orchestrator verification, leveraging the APIs developed in Story A.

### 2. JSON Schema validation gap for custom policy types creates a weaker data contract.

**Impact:** Inconsistent validation robustness between built-in and custom templates, potential for invalid custom template YAMLs to be accepted without clear external validation signals, confusion for users, and violation of the "Data contracts fail loudly" project principle.
**Source:** Technical Alignment, Disaster Prevention (Technical Specification Disasters)

**Problem:**
The story explicitly notes that `scenario-template.schema.json` has a hardcoded `policy_type` enum and considers simply documenting that custom types won't be validated by JSON Schema. This deviates from the project's robust data contract philosophy (`Data contracts fail loudly`) and the use of `jsonschema` for validation, creating an inconsistent and less reliable validation experience for custom templates.

**Recommended Fix:**
Make an explicit decision to extend the JSON Schema (`scenario-template.schema.json`) to accommodate dynamically registered custom policy types. This could involve generating parts of the schema at runtime based on registered types, or introducing a mechanism for custom types to provide their own schema fragments. This ensures that custom templates benefit from the same level of external (e.g., IDE) and runtime `jsonschema` validation as built-in templates, upholding the project's data contract principles. Document this decision and implementation approach clearly.

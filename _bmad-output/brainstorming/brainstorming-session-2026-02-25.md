# Brainstorming Session Results

**Facilitator:** Lucas
**Date:** 2026-02-25

## Session Overview

**Topic:** Top-level product name for an economic modeling platform that unifies microsimulation, macroeconomic models, and specialized policy assessment tools
**Goals:** Generate compelling, memorable name candidates that convey platform breadth, the bridging role between policy and economics, and professional credibility

### Context Guidance

_Platform vision: hosts multiple economic model types (micro, macro, specialized) and serves as a bridge between policy assessment and economic modeling. Inspired by names like PolicyEngine and ScenarioBuilder — compound, clear, professional. Name should reflect exploration/analysis, not policy creation._

### Session Setup

_Approach: AI-Recommended (Streamlined Morphological Analysis) — one-phase blitz combining grid-based component generation with rapid iterative refinement._

## Technique Execution Results

**Morphological Analysis — Grid Dimensions:**

- **Action Verbs:** Simulate, Model, Explore, Assess, Analyze, Bridge, Navigate, Project, Forecast, Map, Scope, Calibrate, Trace, Orbit
- **Domain Nouns:** Policy, Economy, Fiscal, Impact, Systems, Markets, Dynamics, Equilibrium, Flows, Futures, Civic, Reform, Social, Commons, Govern
- **Platform Words:** Engine, Lab, Studio, Hub, Scope, Space, Forge, Dock, Atlas, Nexus, Core, Lens, Grid, Base, Port, Foundry, Works, Mill

### Creative Journey

**Wave 1-3 (Compound + Bold + Single-word):** Initial exploration across PolicyScope, EconLab, ImpactAtlas, Equilibria, Confluent, Parallax, Prism, Simulo, Prospero, Orbis, Tessera, Canopée — established preferences for strong metaphors with professional tone.

**Key Pivot — PolicyForge:** Lucas identified an attraction to "Policy + strong metaphor" with Forge energy (creation, craftsmanship, substance). However, too close to PolicyEngine.

**Wave 4-6 (PolicyForge adjacent):** PolicyLab, PolicyGrid, PolicyOrbit, PolicyNexus, PolicyVerse, FiscalForge, EconForge, ModelForge, PolicyWorks, PolicyCraft, PolicyFoundry, **PolicyStudio** — Studio resonated strongly but still in PolicyEngine's shadow.

**Key Pivot — Beyond "Policy":** Lucas recognized "Policy" was too narrow for a platform hosting macro models, microsimulations, and specialized tools. Needed a word that says "public policy / societal impact" without being literal.

**Semantic Exploration:** Fiscal, Civic, Social, Reform, Welfare, Commons, Govern, Public — mapped the full synonym space around "public policy."

**Wave 11-16 (New first words):** CivicStudio, CivicForge, ReformLab, ReformStudio, ReformScope, CommonsLab, GovStudio, GovernaLab, SocialScope, SocioLab, LevierLab, ImpactStudio, PublicaLab, CitizenScope

### Decision

**Winner: ReformLab**

**Why it works:**
- **Reform** — immediately communicates "public policy change" without being literal
- **Lab** — academic credibility, experimentation, multi-instrument, open-source ethos
- A place where you test reforms before they happen — exactly the product vision
- Two words, four syllables, easy to say and remember
- Works across languages (English, French, German, Spanish)
- Clear of existing major product names
- Broad enough to host microsimulations, macroeconomic models, and specialized tools

**Runner-up: ReformStudio** — strong but "Studio" leans creative/design while "Lab" leans scientific, better matching the simulation + academic audience.

### Session Highlights

**User Creative Strengths:** Strong editorial instinct — quick to identify what works and why, and decisive about killing near-misses (PolicyForge too close to PolicyEngine, PolicyStudio still in the shadow). Excellent at articulating the semantic gap ("we need to understand it's public policies").

**Breakthrough Moment:** The shift from "Policy" to "Reform" — unlocked by reframing the question from "what synonym for policy?" to "what word captures what policy analysis actually does?"

**Energy Flow:** Fast and focused. Lucas preferred efficiency over exhaustive exploration, leading to a streamlined single-technique blitz that reached a strong conclusion in ~15 minutes.

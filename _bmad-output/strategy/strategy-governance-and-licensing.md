# Governance, Licensing & Sustainability Strategy

**Date:** 2026-03-03 | **Status:** Active

## Governance Model — Staged Evolution

| Stage | Timeline | Legal Structure | License | Revenue |
|-------|----------|----------------|---------|---------|
| 1 — Build | Now | Auto-entrepreneur (NAF 7490B) + nom commercial "ReformLab" | AGPL v3 | Grants only |
| 2 — First traction | H2 2026 | AE + Open Collective for community funding | AGPL v3 | Grants + consulting/training |
| 3 — Established | 2027–2028 | Decide based on traction: stay AE, SASU, Association, or SCIC | AGPL core; open-core frontend only if competitive pressure materializes | Grants + consulting + institutional SaaS |

## Licensing Decisions

- **AGPL v3 on everything** (engine + future frontend). Apache 2.0 was switched out because it allowed proprietary forks without contribution.
- **Retain dual-licensing right** as sole copyright holder — commercial license option for institutions that can't use copyleft.
- **"Open until proven otherwise"** — can tighten from open to proprietary for new code, but not the reverse.
- Frontend licensing revisited when competitive pressure is measurable.

## Revenue Architecture

| Segment | What they pay for | How they pay |
|---------|-------------------|--------------|
| Researchers & Academics | Free tool; paid consulting/training | Research grants, project budgets |
| Government administrations | Hosted SaaS + maintained scenarios + support | Public procurement, consulting contracts |
| International organizations | Custom country configs, methodology partnerships | Project contracts (€800–2000+/day) |
| NGOs & advocacy | Ready-made scenario reports, on-demand analysis | Campaign/grant budgets |

**Core insight:** Nobody pays for the software — everybody pays for what you do WITH it. The tool is a credibility engine.

## Strategic Insights

1. **Competitor Paradox:** If the market is too small for confident commercial play, it's too small for competitors to invest in forking.
2. **Concentrated market:** Few institutional buyers, significant budgets, long procurement cycles. Grants ARE the business model, not a backup.
3. **Open-source funding flywheel:** AGPL + open = adoption + trust = grant eligibility = SaaS opportunity.
4. **Moat:** Domain expertise + maintained data + being the recognized reference. Not proprietary code.
